## 1. Problem Statement

Modern AI agent systems are increasingly capable of end-to-end autonomous task completion. However, blindly removing human oversight creates real risks: factual errors, hallucinations, misaligned outputs, and loss of accountability. This assignment demonstrates — through both the article content and the pipeline itself — that Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) design is a deliberate architectural choice, not a limitation.

The challenge is to build a multi-agent pipeline that:
- Produces a high-quality, ~15-page academic article.
- Generates supporting visuals and structured content programmatically.
- Compiles a publication-ready PDF using LaTeX.
- Remains readable, maintainable, and auditable by the course instructors.

## 3. Goals

1. **G1** — Produce a coherent ~15-page academic article on the HITL topic.
2. **G2** — Demonstrate a working CrewAI multi-agent pipeline with at least 5 specialized agents.
3. **G3** — Generate all required article elements: cover page, TOC, figures, tables, formulas, BiDi section, bibliography.
4. **G4** — Compile the article to a polished PDF using XeLaTeX (required for Hebrew/BiDi support).
5. **G5** — Keep all source code modular, documented, and under 150 lines per file.
6. **G6** — Submit the final PDF as `salareen-ex03.pdf`.

---

## 4. Non-Goals

- This is not a production AI system; scalability and throughput are out of scope.
- Real-time or interactive HITL interruption of the CrewAI crew is out of scope (the article discusses it; the pipeline may simulate it).
- Deployment, containerization, or CI/CD pipelines are out of scope.
- The article does not need to be peer-reviewed or published.

---

## 5. Functional Requirements

### FR-1: Multi-Agent Pipeline
- The system must define at least 5 CrewAI agents: Research, Outline, Writer, Reviewer, LaTeX.
- Each agent must have a distinct role, goal, backstory, and set of tasks.
- Agents must pass structured context between each other.

### FR-2: Article Content
- The article must cover: definition of HITL, motivations, architectural patterns, case studies, limitations, and future directions.
- Minimum length: ~15 pages in the compiled PDF.
- Must include a bibliography with at least 8 cited sources.

### FR-3: Required Document Elements
- Cover page with title, authors, date, and course name.
- Automatically generated Table of Contents.
- Page headers and footers.
- At least one image (can be a royalty-free diagram or illustration).
- At least one figure generated by a Python script (e.g., matplotlib).
- At least one structured table.
- At least one mathematical formula (inline or display).
- At least one section demonstrating Hebrew-English BiDi text.
- Linked bibliography entries.

### FR-4: LaTeX Compilation
- The LaTeX source must compile without errors using `xelatex`.
- The output PDF must be `results/salareen-ex03.pdf`.

### FR-5: Configuration
- All agent/task parameters must be configurable via YAML files in `config/`.
- API keys must be loaded from `.env` (never hardcoded).
